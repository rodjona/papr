About the Program

An intensive 18-month pathway into perfusion technology.

The program combines cardiovascular sciences, perfusion technology, simulation, laboratory work, and supervised clinical rotations.

Program Design

Didactic and clinical courses are concurrent.

Lectures are taught by PRPA staff, affiliated surgeons, anesthesiologists, perfusionists, and guest lecturers. Surgical schedules may affect lecture and exam timing.

View Curriculum

Clinical Commitment

Students may be required to be present evenings, nights, weekends, and holidays. Call responsibilities may occur in week-long segments.

Scope of Training

Cardiopulmonary bypass, autotransfusion, plasma exchange, ECMO, cardiopulmonary function, and open-heart operating room experience.

Accreditation Note

PRPA is pursuing accreditation through CAAHEP upon recommendation of ACPE.

Program goals

Clinical Excellence

  • Provide excellence in perfusion education.
  • Develop clinical competence.
  • Promote patient safety and evidence-based decision-making.
  • Advance ECMO and extracorporeal life support education.

Professional Growth

  • Foster professionalism and ethical practice.
  • Encourage lifelong learning and research.
  • Support workforce development in Puerto Rico, the Caribbean, and the USA.
  • Build strategic healthcare partnerships.
